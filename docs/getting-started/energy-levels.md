# Energy Levels

Every show starts with an energy check. Before you plan a single Act, the Writer's Room asks: **"How's your energy today?"**

This isn't a test. There's no right answer. Your energy level shapes the entire lineup: how many Acts get scheduled, what kind of work goes on stage, how many Beats count as a win.

## The Four Levels

### High

| | |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | 8+ hours |
| **Beats possible** | 3-4 |
| **Best for** | Technical work, deep focus, intense prep, creative sprints |

You woke up and your brain is online. This is a day for the hard stuff: the projects that need sustained concentration, the problems that require holding multiple things in your head at once. The lineup will be longer, the Acts will lean toward deep work, and the Beat threshold will be higher.

High energy days aren't the standard. They're a gift when they show up.

### Medium

| | |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | 6-8 hours |
| **Beats possible** | 2-3 |
| **Best for** | Mix of technical and admin, meetings, steady-state work |

A solid working day. You can focus, but maybe not for marathon stretches. The lineup mixes focused work with lighter tasks. This is probably your most common energy level, and it produces perfectly good shows.

### Low

| | |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | 4-6 hours |
| **Beats possible** | 1-2 |
| **Best for** | Admin, light prep, review, emails, errands |

The tank isn't full today. That's fine. The lineup will be shorter and weighted toward tasks that don't demand deep concentration. One or two Beats is a great outcome on a low energy day. The show adapts to you.

### Recovery

| | |
|---|---|
| **Capacity** | 2-4 hours |
| **Beats possible** | 1 Beat = win |
| **Best for** | Gentle activities only: light reading, a walk, basic tidying |

Some days the battery is nearly empty. Maybe you slept badly. Maybe yesterday was enormous. Maybe your brain just isn't cooperating. Recovery isn't a lesser version of Low. It's its own thing.

On a Recovery day, a single Beat is the win threshold. One moment of presence in the entire day, and you've won. The lineup will be short and gentle. Anything beyond that is bonus.

## Energy Isn't Judgment

Picking "Recovery" doesn't mean you're failing. Picking "High" doesn't mean you're performing well. Energy is a weather report, not a grade. You're describing conditions, not evaluating yourself.

Some things worth knowing:

- **Your energy can change mid-show.** If you picked Medium and the day is going worse than expected, the Director is there to help you adjust. You can always call the show early or take a longer intermission.

- **Recovery days happen to everyone.** They happen more often with ADHD brains, and that's not a character flaw. A Recovery day with one locked Beat is a won show.

- **You don't owe anyone a High energy day.** Not the app, not the framework, not yourself. The show runs at whatever level you bring to it.

- **Consistency isn't the goal.** Three Recovery days in a row followed by a High day is a perfectly valid week. The framework doesn't track streaks. Each show stands on its own.

The energy check exists so the framework can build you a show that fits today. Not yesterday's show. Not the show you think you should be running. Today's show, for today's you.
