The chip appears beside the timer at 64px size — never obscuring the countdown itself. It pulses gently (amber) or more urgently (red) when drift is increasing, then settles to static when drift stabilizes.

Design Notes

  • The drift indicator sits between the timer and beat stars in the pill layout.
  • When on time (≤ 2 min), shows only a small green dot (3px) to avoid clutter.
  • When behind, compact text like "+12m" appears in amber or red at 10px mono.
  • When ahead, "-5m" appears in green — a small reward signal.
  • The pill width stays at 320px; drift text compresses the act name (which already truncates).

Philosophy

Why This Works for ADHD

Ambient, not anxious

Drift information is always there when you look — but it never screams. The pill shows a tiny "+12m" in amber. The expanded view puts a chip next to the timer. You absorb it without stress because it's presented as information, not judgment.

Story, not score

The Schedule Story at Strike doesn't say "you ran 15 minutes late." It says "Act 2 ran 10 minutes over — creative momentum." Drift is reframed as the natural rhythm of a live show. Some sketches run long. That's television.

Recovery is celebrated

When you finish an act early or take a shorter break, the green delta chips feel like small wins. "You recovered 3 minutes during Act 4" is a dopamine hit. Natural self-correction is visible and rewarding.

No new views required

Every enhancement is additive to existing views. The pill, expanded view, lineup, and strike all keep their current structure. Drift is layered in, not bolted on. Removing it later is trivial — it's purely decorative information.

Drift Color Semantics

On time or ahead ≤ 2 min behind, or any amount ahead
on time -5m
Slightly behind 3-10 minutes behind schedule
+8m
Significantly behind > 10 minutes behind — consider adjusting

Note: Red drift does not trigger Director Mode automatically. It's information, not an alarm. The user can choose to engage Director Mode if they want to reshape the rest of the show.
