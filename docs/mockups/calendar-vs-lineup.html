Calendar vs Lineup — UX Exploration

Calendar events are read-only anchors from Google Calendar. The Lineup is the show plan Claude builds around them. Three approaches to keeping them visually distinct.

Approach A — Stacked Sections

Calendar appears as a read-only "Your Schedule" section at the top. The Lineup appears below as a separate "Tonight's Show" section. Clear visual distinction through color coding: blue for calendar, warm tones for lineup.

+ Clearest separation. Calendar can't be accidentally reordered.
- Doesn't show how lineup fits around calendar in time.

Design Notes

  • Calendar section is blue-tinted, read-only, lock icon on each event
  • Lineup section is warm-toned, draggable, editable
  • User sees their commitments first, then what Claude planned around them
  • Calendar events never show drag handles — they're anchors, not acts
  • "Finalize Lineup" only applies to the show acts, not calendar events

Approach B — Unified Timeline

A single chronological timeline where calendar events and lineup acts interleave. Calendar events have blue left-border + "FIXED" badge. Acts have category colors + drag handles. Shows exactly how the lineup fills gaps between your commitments.

+ Shows the full picture of your day in order. Most informative.
- More complex. Risk of confusion between what's editable vs fixed.

Design Notes

  • Calendar events: blue dot, blue left-border, "FIXED" badge, no drag handles
  • Lineup acts: category-colored dot, drag handles, editable
  • Timeline shows the real flow of the day — how acts weave around commitments
  • Acts can only be reordered within the gaps between calendar events
  • Time column gives at-a-glance orientation

Approach C — Side-by-Side Panels

Calendar as a narrow reference panel on the left. Lineup as the main panel on the right. Calendar is always visible context while you edit the lineup. Most compact — fits in the Writer's Room's 560px width.

+ Calendar is always visible reference. Lineup has full editing space.
- Tight at 560px. Doesn't explicitly show time relationships.
