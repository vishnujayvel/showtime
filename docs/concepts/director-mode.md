# Director Mode

Every live show has a Director -- the person in the control room making calls when things go off-script. In Showtime, the Director is a mode you can step into when the day isn't going as planned. The Director is not a judge. The Director is the person who keeps the show running.

## When Director Mode appears

Director Mode can be triggered when:

- You're feeling overwhelmed
- You've skipped two or more Acts in a row
- Anxiety is building and the lineup feels impossible
- You don't know what to do next
- The day has gone sideways and you need to regroup

You can also open Director Mode yourself at any time. You don't have to wait for the app to suggest it.

## The six options

Director Mode presents six choices. All of them are valid. None of them is failure.

### Edit the lineup

*"The plan needs adjusting."*

Sometimes the lineup you built this morning doesn't match this afternoon's reality. Meetings got added, energy shifted, priorities changed. The Director can reopen the lineup for editing — add acts, remove acts, reorder the sequence — without resetting the whole show.

This is the most common Director action. It's not a sign the original plan was bad. It's a sign the Director is paying attention.

### Skip to next Act

*"This one isn't landing. Move on."*

Sometimes an Act just doesn't work. The energy isn't matching the task. Your focus drifted and never came back. The topic changed since this morning. Whatever the reason, the Director's call is simple: cut this Act from the show and move to the next one.

The skipped Act isn't a failure -- it's a segment that got cut from tonight's broadcast. It happens in every live show. The audience (that's you) barely notices.

### Call the show early

*"Some shows are shorter. That's okay."*

You can end the entire show right now. No more Acts, no more timers. The show moves directly to Strike the Stage, where you'll get your verdict based on what you did accomplish.

Calling the show early is a production decision. A director wraps a taping when the conditions aren't right. A network pulls a broadcast when the situation calls for it. You're making the same kind of call, and it takes more self-awareness than pushing through on autopilot.

### Take a longer break

*"Extended intermission. No guilt."*

A regular Intermission is the natural pause between Acts. A longer break is the Director saying: you need more time, and that's fine. Step away. Go for a walk. Take a nap. Do whatever you need.

There is no timer on this break. There is no limit. Rest costs zero, and that's true whether the break is five minutes or two hours.

### Just breathe

*"Five minutes of nothing."*

Not a break. Not an intermission. Just five minutes where the only instruction is to breathe. The screen goes calm. The clock pauses. Nothing is expected of you.

Sometimes the ADHD brain doesn't need a strategy or a pivot. It needs five minutes where nobody -- including itself -- is asking it to do anything.

### Reset today's show

*"Start fresh. Clean slate."*

Sometimes the show has gone so far off-script that patching it isn't the right call. The Director can reset the entire show — clear the lineup, reset the timer, wipe the beats — and start over from Dark Studio.

This requires confirmation (it can't be undone). But it's there because sometimes the most compassionate thing is a clean start, not a rescue mission.

## Why this works

Director Mode is grounded in self-determination theory: the idea that autonomy in choosing how to handle difficulty increases motivation rather than depleting it.

When a productivity tool forces you to either "keep going" or "give up," it strips away your agency. You're either succeeding or failing, with nothing in between. Director Mode gives you four distinct paths forward, each one framed as a legitimate choice made by someone in control -- not a concession made by someone who couldn't hack it.

The difference matters. "I called the show early" is a sentence spoken by someone who made a decision. "I gave up" is a sentence spoken by someone who was defeated. Same outcome, completely different emotional residue.

::: tip You are always the Director
Director Mode isn't just for emergencies. If you wake up and know it's going to be a short day, you can plan a two-Act show in the Writer's Room and call it after that. The Director makes the call before the show starts, not just when things fall apart.
:::
