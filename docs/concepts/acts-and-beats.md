# Acts & Beats

Acts and Beats are the building blocks of every show. Acts define what you do and for how long. Beats define whether you were actually there while doing it.

## Acts

An Act is a time-boxed block of focused execution. You're not working on a "task" -- you're performing an Act in tonight's show.

### Duration

Acts run 45 to 90 minutes. This range is deliberate:

- **45 minutes** is long enough for meaningful work but short enough that the ADHD brain doesn't panic at the commitment.
- **90 minutes** is the ceiling. No Act runs longer. When the timer hits zero, the Act is over -- whether the underlying work is "done" or not. The Act is done because the time is done.

### Sketches

Every Act has a Sketch -- its category. Sketches help the Writer's Room build a balanced lineup and give each Act visual identity through color-coded clapperboard badges.

| Sketch | Color | Examples |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Deep Work** | Purple | Coding, writing, design, focused problem-solving |
| **Exercise** | Green | Gym, run, yoga, walk |
| **Admin** | Blue | Email, scheduling, paperwork, errands |
| **Creative** | Amber | Brainstorming, art, music, side projects |
| **Social** | Pink | Meetings, calls, catch-ups, collaboration |

The lineup is shaped by your morning energy check. High energy days get more Deep Work acts. Recovery days lean toward Admin and gentle activities. The plan meets you where you are.

### What happens when an Act ends

When the studio clock hits zero:

1. The timer stops.
2. A **Beat Check** appears -- a spotlight narrows and asks: *"Did you have a moment of presence?"*
3. You either lock a Beat or skip it.
4. The show moves to **Intermission** before the next Act.

An Act doesn't need to be "completed" in the productivity sense. You don't have to finish the task perfectly. You showed up, you performed the Act, and the time is honored.

## Beats

A Beat is not productivity. A Beat is presence.

### What a Beat is

A Beat happens when you notice what you're doing. When you're grounded in the moment. When you feel alive, proud, or immersed -- even briefly.

- *"I am here, doing the hard thing."*
- *"This solution is actually clicking."*
- *"I noticed I was focused, and that felt good."*
- *"This rep matters."*
- *"I'm enjoying this."*

A Beat is the difference between going through the motions and being alive inside the work. It doesn't require finishing anything. It doesn't require perfection. It requires noticing.

### What a Beat is NOT

- Completing a task mechanically
- Grinding through without presence
- Finishing just to finish
- Checking a box

You can complete an Act without having a Beat. That's fine. The Act still counted. You still showed up. But an Act with a Beat is something more -- it means you were there, not just your body at the desk, but you.

### Locking a Beat

After each Act, the Beat Check modal appears. The spotlight narrows. The question is simple: *"Did you have a moment of presence?"*

If yes, lock the Beat. A golden star ignites -- the `beatIgnite` animation transitions from dim gray to warm gold in 0.6 seconds. *"That moment was real."*

If no, that's okay. Tap skip. No judgment. The show continues.

### Beat Stars

Your Beat count is always visible:

- **Gold** &#9733; -- a locked Beat. You were there.
- **Gray** &#9734; -- an empty slot. No Beat locked for that Act.

The stars appear in the pill view, the expanded view, and the Strike summary. They're a gentle record of presence, not a score.

### Win Threshold

The default win threshold is **3 Beats per day**. Lock three moments of presence across your entire show and the day is won.

The threshold adjusts with your energy level:

| Energy | Beat Threshold | What this means |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| **High** | 3-4 Beats | A full day of presence opportunities |
| **Medium** | 2-3 Beats | A solid lineup with room to land |
| **Low** | 1-2 Beats | A lighter show. Still winnable. |
| **Recovery** | 1 Beat | One moment of presence = the day is won |

On a Recovery day, a single Beat -- one moment where you noticed you were alive inside the work -- is enough for a standing ovation. That's not a consolation prize. That's the design.
