# Verdicts

At Strike the Stage, the show is over and you receive a verdict. Verdicts are not grades. They don't rank your day on a scale from A to F. They celebrate what you did -- and every single one of them acknowledges that you showed up.

## The Four Verdicts

### DAY WON

**Condition:** You hit the Beat threshold.

The golden glow animation pulses. Large bold text. Standing ovation.

You showed up, you performed your Acts, and you were *present* for enough of them. This is not about completing every task on a checklist. It's about being alive inside your day. Three moments of genuine presence (or whatever your energy-adjusted threshold was) -- that's a day won.

### SOLID SHOW

**Condition:** You completed most of your Acts and came close to the Beat threshold.

A strong performance. One Beat short, maybe, but the show ran well. The lineup got executed. You were on stage doing the work. This is a genuinely good day -- the kind most people would be proud of, and you should be too.

### GOOD EFFORT

**Condition:** You got on stage. You did what you could.

Some days are harder than others. Maybe the energy wasn't there. Maybe life interrupted. Maybe the Acts didn't land the way you hoped. You still opened the app. You still stepped onto the stage. You still tried. That matters more than most productivity tools will ever tell you.

### SHOW CALLED EARLY

**Condition:** You used Director Mode to end the show before the lineup was finished.

This is not failure. Read that again: **this is not failure.**

Sometimes the show gets called early. That's showbiz. A network pulls the plug on a broadcast. A director wraps a taping ahead of schedule. A performer says "I need to stop." All of these are legitimate production decisions, and so is yours.

SHOW CALLED EARLY means you recognized where you were, you made a conscious choice, and you closed the show with intention instead of letting it dissolve into guilt.

## What's missing from this list

There is no "BAD SHOW" verdict. There is no "FAILED" state. There is no red. There is no F.

The worst possible outcome in Showtime -- calling the show early on a Recovery day -- is still framed as a valid production decision made by the Director, not a personal failing by the Performer.

This is not participation-trophy thinking. It's recognition that the hardest part of an ADHD day is often just starting. If you opened the app, you already did something most productivity tools never acknowledge.

## Why verdicts work this way

Most productivity tools measure completion: tasks done, streaks maintained, checkboxes filled. When you inevitably miss a day or fall short, the tool shows you a red number, a broken streak, a dead tree. The message is clear: you failed.

Showtime measures presence instead. The verdict doesn't ask "did you finish everything?" It asks "were you there?" And it frames every possible answer with the language of live production -- a world where shows get called early, lineups get reshuffled, and the crew still goes out for drinks after.

::: info A new show every day
Verdicts don't carry over. Tomorrow's show has no memory of today's verdict. There's no weekly average, no running score, no streak. Tomorrow the stage is empty and the Writer's Room is ready. Every show stands on its own.
:::
